Hikkaduwa.
Turtle Hatchery

Turtle Hatchery is located on the beach, roughly 2 km north of Hikkaduwa. A small and private research center that aims to protect the endangered marine turtles, the hatchery offers a selective display of turtle eggs and exhibits the progressive development of turtles. There are some spots where you can also get an opportunity to release baby turtles into the vast ocean for an additional fee. The best time to visit here and see the turtles is March through April.

Relax in Hikkaduwa Beach

Hikkaduwa Beach, a popular tourist hotspot since the 1960s, offers pristine landscapes and alluring waters for recreation, leisure, and relaxation. Water sports like snorkeling, diving, and surfing attract adrenaline junkies, while family picnics and beach volleyball provide endless fun.

Walk around Hikkaduwa National Park

Hikkaduwa National Park, one of Sri Lanka's three marine national parks, is a kaleidoscopic attraction with a reef containing coral and marine species. It protects the Hikkaduwa Beach from erosion, making it a significant attraction on the island's southern coast.
